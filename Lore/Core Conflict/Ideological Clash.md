# The Ideological Clash

For centuries, tension brewed between the two dominant forces of the world, defined entirely by how they view wealth, trade, and survival.

## The Merchant Aristocrats
[[The High Table]] values prosperity more than anything else. To them, the entire world is a business landscape, anyone and everyone is viewed as a possible client and a valuable source of income. Their military forces were historically deployed to protect trade routes and safeguard the immense wealth flowing into their floating sky domains. They are not a faction that seeks out bloody wars for glory, but they will ruthlessly protect their bottom line. 

## The Perpetual Raiders
In stark contrast, [[The Umbral Vanguards]] are a decentralized, barbaric faction of mountain pirates and survivalists who reject the crown's authority. They thrive in the **Dusk Redwood forests**, harsh environments dominated by giant, towering trees that block out the sun, leaving the forest floor in a state of constant dim light and thick fog. Because they cannot grow or trade traditional wealth in these dark conditions, they live off constant pillaging. To the Vanguards, warfare is their economy.

## The Tipping Point of Trade
For generations, The High Table tolerated the Vanguards' regional raiding. They viewed the mountain pirates as an unfortunate, but manageable, cost of doing business, primarily because the raids only had minor impacts on surface trades. 

That corporate tolerance shattered completely when the Vanguards wiped out the [[Lost Civilization]]. By annihilating that oceanic empire, the Vanguards permanently erased one of the High Table's most lucrative trading partner, completely cutting off the merchant lords from invaluable deep-sea commerce and rare materials. Except for the Umbral Vanguard, everyone was a potential client; now, the Vanguards are targets for total elimination.
